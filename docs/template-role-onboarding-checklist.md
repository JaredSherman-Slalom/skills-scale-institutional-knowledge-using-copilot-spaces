# Role Onboarding Checklist Template

## Purpose
This checklist ensures new team members in any role have a structured onboarding experience, understand their responsibilities, and can quickly become productive contributors.

---

## General Onboarding (All Roles)

### Week 1: Orientation
- [ ] Review OctoAcme project management overview
- [ ] Read role-specific section in [Roles & Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md)
- [ ] Understand project principles and values
- [ ] Access granted to required tools and systems (GitHub, project boards, communication channels)
- [ ] Introduction meeting with project sponsor/leader
- [ ] Meet the team and understand team structure
- [ ] Review current project charter and key objectives

### Week 2: Context Building
- [ ] Review existing project artifacts (backlog, roadmap, risk register)
- [ ] Understand current project phase and recent milestones
- [ ] Shadow relevant team ceremonies (standups, planning, reviews)
- [ ] Read recent retrospective notes and improvement actions
- [ ] Identify key stakeholders and communication channels
- [ ] Review escalation paths and support processes

### Week 3-4: Active Participation
- [ ] Take on initial responsibilities with mentor support
- [ ] Participate actively in role-specific ceremonies
- [ ] Establish regular sync cadence with key collaborators
- [ ] Provide feedback on onboarding experience
- [ ] Identify any gaps in documentation or process

---

## Role-Specific Onboarding

### For Developers
- [ ] Set up local development environment
- [ ] Review codebase architecture and key components
- [ ] Complete first small PR (bug fix or documentation update)
- [ ] Understand branching strategy and PR workflow
- [ ] Review testing approach and coverage standards
- [ ] Participate in code review as observer and reviewer
- [ ] Pair program with experienced team member

### For Project Managers
- [ ] Review and understand current project plan and timeline
- [ ] Understand risk register and active mitigation plans
- [ ] Review stakeholder communication history and expectations
- [ ] Set up status reporting cadence and templates
- [ ] Understand budget and resource constraints
- [ ] Review dependency map and cross-team coordination needs
- [ ] Schedule handoff meeting with previous PM (if applicable)

### For Product Managers
- [ ] Review product vision, strategy, and roadmap
- [ ] Understand customer personas and key use cases
- [ ] Review success metrics and current performance
- [ ] Meet with key stakeholders and customers
- [ ] Understand competitive landscape and market position
- [ ] Review product backlog and prioritization approach
- [ ] Schedule handoff with previous PM (if applicable)

### For Scrum Masters
- [ ] Observe all sprint ceremonies for one full sprint
- [ ] Understand team velocity and historical performance
- [ ] Review impediment log and resolution patterns
- [ ] Meet with team members individually to understand challenges
- [ ] Review team working agreements and norms
- [ ] Understand organizational agile maturity and support
- [ ] Connect with Agile Coach (if available) for guidance

### For Product Owners
- [ ] Review and understand current product backlog
- [ ] Shadow previous PO in sprint planning and reviews
- [ ] Meet with key stakeholders to understand priorities
- [ ] Review acceptance criteria patterns and quality standards
- [ ] Understand Definition of Done and quality gates
- [ ] Review user feedback and feature requests
- [ ] Establish availability pattern for team questions

### For QA Leads
- [ ] Review current test strategy and test plans
- [ ] Understand test automation framework and coverage
- [ ] Review quality metrics and defect trends
- [ ] Set up test environment access and tools
- [ ] Understand Definition of Done and quality gates
- [ ] Review recent production incidents related to quality
- [ ] Meet with development team to discuss testability

### For Technical Architects
- [ ] Review architecture documentation and diagrams
- [ ] Understand technical debt and improvement priorities
- [ ] Review architecture decision records (ADRs)
- [ ] Understand non-functional requirements and constraints
- [ ] Review security and compliance requirements
- [ ] Meet with senior developers to discuss technical challenges
- [ ] Understand technology stack and infrastructure

### For Agile Coaches
- [ ] Assess current agile maturity of teams and organization
- [ ] Review historical retrospectives and improvement actions
- [ ] Meet with leadership to understand transformation goals
- [ ] Observe multiple teams to understand practices and challenges
- [ ] Review organizational impediments and patterns
- [ ] Connect with Scrum Masters and team leads
- [ ] Develop initial coaching plan

### For Change Managers
- [ ] Review change management history and lessons learned
- [ ] Understand impacted stakeholder groups and change readiness
- [ ] Review current product roadmap and upcoming changes
- [ ] Develop stakeholder communication strategy
- [ ] Assess training needs and existing materials
- [ ] Understand organizational change capacity
- [ ] Meet with support and operations teams

## Handoff Meetings (when replacing someone)

### Handoff Meeting Agenda
1. **Context**: Project history, key decisions, lessons learned
2. **Current State**: Active work, blockers, risks
3. **Key Relationships**: Stakeholder map, communication preferences
4. **Open Items**: Pending decisions, escalations, commitments
5. **Resources**: Where to find information, who to ask for help
6. **Tips**: What worked well, what to watch out for

### Post-Handoff
- [ ] Document key takeaways from handoff discussion
- [ ] Schedule follow-up questions session (1-2 weeks out)
- [ ] Ensure previous role holder is available for transition period
- [ ] Update stakeholders on role transition and new contact

---

## Success Indicators

By the end of onboarding (typically 4-6 weeks), you should be able to:
- ✓ Understand your responsibilities and decision authority
- ✓ Know who to collaborate with and how
- ✓ Navigate project artifacts and documentation independently
- ✓ Perform core role functions with minimal guidance
- ✓ Contribute to team ceremonies and discussions effectively
- ✓ Identify and escalate issues appropriately

---

## Onboarding Feedback

After completing onboarding, provide feedback on:
- What worked well in the onboarding process?
- What was confusing or missing?
- How could the onboarding be improved?
- What resources were most helpful?

Document feedback for continuous improvement of this onboarding process.
